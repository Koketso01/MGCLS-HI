Help & User Guide

This page explains how to use the MGCLS–H I database, how to interpret the cluster and galaxy catalogues, and how the main physical quantities are defined. It is intended for scientifically literate users (students, researchers and data scientists) who want to work directly with the released products.

1.1. Cluster search

On the Home and Search pages you can start from the cluster catalogue:

  • Use the cluster name / ID field to search by MGCLS name (e.g. Abell-194, j012547-012223) or other identifiers exposed in the interface.
  • Use filters such as redshift, R200, or velocity range where available to narrow down the field.
  • The results table links to a cluster detail page with preview figures, basic parameters and links to download the associated products.

1.2. Galaxy / H I source search

Once a cluster is selected, you can explore the associated H I detections:

  • The galaxy table lists all H I sources detected in the cube for that cluster field.
  • You can sort and filter by quantities such as vrad, line width, integrated H I flux, or H I mass (if provided).
  • RA/Dec columns provide the sky position of each detection; velocities are in km s−1 and can be converted to redshift or used for phase–space diagrams.

1.3. Preview figures and quick-look science

Each cluster page includes multi-wavelength previews (radio, optical, X-ray and overlays). These are intended as quick-look diagnostics:

  • Identify interesting systems (e.g. ram-pressure stripped galaxies, tails, H I holes).
  • Visually inspect the H I morphology relative to the cluster ICM and galaxy distribution.
  • Check the footprint and depth before downloading full cubes or cubelets.

1.4. Downloading data products

For each cluster, the database provides downloadable packages, typically as compressed archives (ZIP or similar):

  • Cluster-level H I cubes and moment maps.
  • Per-cluster H I catalogues (cluster table and galaxy table).
  • Galaxy cubelets and associated diagnostic products.

Once downloaded, the FITS products can be analysed with common astronomy tools (e.g. Python/astropy, CASA, your own pipelines).

The cluster catalogue describes each MGCLS–H I field: sky position, observing setup and basic dynamical properties. The physical quantities are written in standard notation, e.g. R200, σv, NHI, M⊙.

The table below describes each column in scientific terms.

Column Units Definition & scientific usage
Cluster (Name/ID) – Cluster / field identifier (e.g. Abell-, RXC-, MACS- or MGCLS-based name). Used as the primary human–readable name for the pointing.
z z Target redshift used to set the H I tuning (dimensionless). This is effectively the cluster redshift assumed when defining the frequency range of the spectral-line observation.
RA HH:MM:SS.S (J2000) Right Ascension of the pointing centre in sexagesimal hours, minutes and seconds (J2000). Combined with Dec it specifies the sky position of the beam centre. Can be converted to decimal degrees for analysis.
DEC DD:MM:SS.S (J2000) Declination of the pointing centre in sexagesimal degrees, arcminutes and arcseconds (J2000).
DATE UTC (ISO 8601) Start date and time of the observation in Coordinated Universal Time, formatted as YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SSZ.
SBID – MeerKAT Schedule Block identifier. Unique ID for the observing block in the telescope scheduling system.
CAPTURE ID – Internal capture identifier for the raw observation in the archive, used when tracing the data back to telescope ingest.
R200 Mpc Radius R200 within which the mean mass density of the cluster is 200 times the critical density of the Universe at the cluster redshift:
⟨ρ(r < R200)⟩ = 200 ρcrit(z).
Often used as a characteristic size of the dark-matter halo and when defining “cluster-centric radius” for phase-space and environmental studies.
σv km s−1 One-dimensional velocity dispersion σv of cluster galaxies, derived from optical redshift measurements. This is a key dynamical indicator and mass proxy; M200 ∝ σvα with α ≈ 3–4 in many calibrations.
Detections count Number of H I sources detected in the primary beam of this cube (after applying the MGCLS–H I selection, quality control and masking).
RMS Jy beam−1 Typical image root-mean-square noise per beam in a representative channel of the H I cube (after continuum subtraction and imaging). Sets the baseline sensitivity used to compute column-density limits.
NHImin, NHImax cm−2 H I column-density sensitivity range of the cube. Column density NHI measures how many neutral hydrogen atoms are present along the line of sight. For optically thin 21-cm emission,
NHI ≈ 1.823 × 1018 ∫ TB(v) dv    cm−2,
where TB is the brightness temperature (K) and dv is velocity in km s−1.

NHImin is the minimum detectable column density, set by the image rms noise and effective velocity resolution:
NHI,min ≈ 1.823 × 1018 · TB,rms · Δv,
where TB,rms is derived from the rms flux density (Jy beam−1) using the beam solid angle. Lower values correspond to deeper, more sensitive cubes capable of detecting faint diffuse gas.

NHImax is an approximate maximum reliably measurable column density before saturation, non-linearities or beam-smearing effects become important. It is usually set by the brightest emission in the field and characterises the usable dynamic range.

Together, the interval [NHImin → NHImax] describes the full range of column densities probed in a given cube.
Vmin, Vmax km s−1 Minimum and maximum barycentric radial velocity covered by the cube. These define the usable H I velocity window and are directly related to the frequency tuning.
NCHAN – Number of spectral channels between Vmin and Vmax. Combined with the velocity range, this sets the channel width and effective spectral resolution.
FREQmin, FREQmax MHz Minimum and maximum sky frequency of the spectral-line band in MHz. The rest frequency of the H I 21-cm line is 1420.4058 MHz; the tuning corresponds roughly to the cluster redshift via ν ≈ 1420.4058 / (1 + z) MHz.
BMIN, BMAJ arcsec Full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the synthesised beam’s minor and major axes. These define the angular resolution of the cube and the smallest structures that can be resolved.
BPA deg Beam position angle (PA) in degrees, measured from north through east. Specifies the orientation of the elliptical synthesised beam on the sky.
MGCLS Field Name – Internal MGCLS short name for the pointing (e.g. j012547-012223), derived from the RA/Dec of the radio source used in the legacy survey.
Mass M⊙ Estimated total cluster mass, typically close to M200 (mass within R200), derived from dynamical or X-ray proxies and expressed in solar masses.

The MGCLS–H I galaxy catalogue is derived from automated 3-D source finding applied to the H I cubes. Each row corresponds to one detected H I source (typically a galaxy) and stores both basic observables and morphological parameters.

MKTCS-HI J054528.05-252543.3      7    86.3669    -25.4287      8.06×10⁶                2.31×10²                1.04×10²            7.69            4.43

The exact values will differ in your catalogue. This row is provided only to illustrate how to read the columns below.

Column Units Definition & scientific usage
Galaxy name (name) – MGCLS–H I source identifier, usually of the form MKTCS-HI JHHMMSS.ss±DDMMSS.s, derived from the J2000 sky position of the H I centroid. Use this as the primary ID when cross-matching with external catalogues.
ID (id) – Internal integer source ID within the cube. Often used to build filenames for cubelets and diagnostic products.
x, y, z pix Flux-weighted centroid of the source in pixel coordinates: x (RA direction), y (Dec direction) and z (spectral channel index). For most science work, users will instead use the corresponding world coordinates ra, dec and v_rad, but the pixel indices are useful if re-analysing the native cubes.
x_min, x_max,
y_min, y_max,
z_min, z_max 		pix Minimum and maximum pixel indices of the 3-D bounding box that contains the detected emission. This defines the region from which flux and morphological parameters are measured.
Smin, Smax (f_min, f_max),
mean, std, rms 		Jy beam−1 Basic statistics of the flux distribution inside the source mask. f_min and f_max are the minimum and maximum voxel values; mean, std and rms characterise the average brightness and noise level within the detected region. These are useful for sanity checks and for understanding how close the detection is to the noise floor.
Sint (f_sum) Jy km s−1 Integrated H I line flux over the full 3-D mask. This is the key observable used to estimate H I mass:
MHI ≈ 2.356 × 105   D2   FHI    M⊙,
where FHI is the integrated line flux in Jy km s−1 and D is the distance in Mpc (derived from redshift and cosmology).
Vrad (v_rad) m s−1 Flux-weighted radial velocity of the source, usually in the same reference frame as the parent cube (e.g. barycentric, radio definition). For many applications you may wish to convert to km s−1 and subtract the cluster systemic velocity to place the galaxy in phase–space.
W20 (w20), W50 (w50),
wm50 		m s−1 Global H I line widths measured at 20% and 50% of the peak, and a robust estimator around 50%. These are sensitive to the projected rotation velocity and can be used as inputs to Tully–Fisher analyses, dynamical mass estimates and to characterise disturbed profiles.
ell_maj, ell_min,
ell_pa 		pix, deg Major and minor axis lengths and position angle (PA) of a 2-D ellipse fitted to the spatial H I distribution, usually measured from north through east. These parameters summarise the projected size and orientation of the source on the sky.
ell3s_maj, ell3s_min,
ell3s_pa 		pix, deg Ellipse parameters measured at a higher significance threshold (e.g. three-sigma isophote), providing a more conservative size estimate that is less sensitive to very low-level emission.
kin_pa deg Estimated kinematic position angle of the rotating H I disk, where measurable. Comparing kin_pa to the morphological PA can reveal warps, lopsided disks or disturbed kinematics.
Right Ascension, Declination (ra, dec) deg (J2000) Sky coordinates (RA, Dec) of the flux-weighted centroid of the detected H I emission, in decimal degrees (J2000). These columns are the recommended ones for cross-matching to optical/IR catalogues.
ra_peak, dec_peak,
v_rad_peak 		deg, deg, m s−1 World coordinates and velocity at the position of maximum H I brightness within the source mask. Useful for checking whether the brightest voxel coincides with the centroid and for exploring asymmetries.
err_x, err_y, err_z,
err_f_sum 		pix, pix, pix, Jy km s−1 Formal uncertainties on the centroid position in pixel units and on the integrated H I flux. These can be propagated into error budgets when estimating H I masses or when comparing to simulations and other surveys.

In practice, many users will primarily work with name, ra, dec, v_rad, the line widths, f_sum and any derived H I masses. The additional morphological and error terms are provided for more detailed structural and kinematic analyses.

Symbol Meaning
z Redshift of the cluster or galaxy.
R200 Radius where the mean enclosed density is 200× the critical density.
σv 1-D velocity dispersion of galaxies in the cluster.
NHI H I column density (atoms cm−2).
MHI Neutral hydrogen mass of a galaxy, in solar masses.
